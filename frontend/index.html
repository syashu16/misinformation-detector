Learn to Spot Misinformation

Common Red Flags

  • Emotional, inflammatory language designed to provoke reactions
  • Claims that "they" don't want you to know something
  • No author byline or contact information
  • Requests to share immediately before "it gets taken down"
  • Extraordinary claims without credible evidence
  • Grammar and spelling errors in professional content

Verification Steps

  1. Check the Source: Research publisher credentials and reputation
  2. Cross-Reference: Verify with multiple reliable sources
  3. Fact-Check: Use Snopes, FactCheck.org, or PolitiFact
  4. Look for Evidence: Check for citations and supporting data
  5. Consider Context: Think about timing and motivation

Quick Tips

Pause Before Sharing: Take a moment to verify before forwarding

Check Your Emotions: Misinformation often triggers strong feelings

Ask Others: Get second opinions from knowledgeable people

Wait for Updates: Breaking news often gets corrected over time
